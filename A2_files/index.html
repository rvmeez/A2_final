Emerging in late 2019, COVID-19 swiftly swept across the globe, impacting communities, healthcare systems, and economies. By 2022, the world had witnessed unprecedented challenges and losses. The following visualization serves as a powerful tool encapsulating this journey. Through clear and compelling charts, this visualization illustrates the pandemic's geographical reach, tracing the of cases and deaths around the world.

Total Deaths in Each Country

This bubble chart visualizes the sobering reality of COVID-19 fatalities worldwide up to 2022. By employing varying bubble sizes, it conveys the scale of lives lost in different regions. This poignant representation offers a compelling insight into the gravity of the pandemic, highlighting the extent of its human toll across nations.

USA records the highest COVID-19 death toll due to its large population, extensive testing, and healthcare data availability.

Conversely, Vanuatu, with a small population and strict containment measures, has significantly lower deaths, reflecting effective preventive strategies and limited virus exposure.
